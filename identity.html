Four Levels of Identity

Four Levels of Identity in Hermetical Astrology

The Four Levels and Three Modes of Identity are generally derived from the seven planets and twelve signs in a hierarchical manner ascending through their elements and polarity. In the personal chart they reveal detailed unique characteristics of the native. In the Hermetic Cosmos, everything is made out of the four cosmic elements of fire, air, water and earth.

These are combined with the creation of each new being. The positions of planets within their zodiacal signs reveal the true nature and to some extent, destiny.

The Four Levels of Identity
In Neoplatonism

The study of the levels of identity was maintained by the philosopher Plotinus in his system of Neoplatonism. For Plotinus and his follower Porphyry, the emanations are as follows:

To Hen (τό ἕν), The One: Deity without quality, sometimes called The Good.
Nous (Νοῦς), Mind: The Universal consciousness, from which proceeds
Psychē (Ψυχή), Soul: Including both individual and world soul, leading finally to
Physis (Φύσις), Nature.
